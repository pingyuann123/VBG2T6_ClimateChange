Impact of Urbanization on Temperature: Does Urban Growth Contribute to Rising Temperatures?

Temperature Trends by Country

Insights

Notable Trends

  • Countries like Brazil, UAE, and India consistently show high temperatures from 1990 to 2022, reflecting the natural climate of tropical and arid regions, where the combination of urbanization and geographic factors leads to sustained high temperatures over time.
  • Urbanized countries, particularly in South Asia and the Middle East, are also seeing increased frequency of temperature extremes, suggesting that urban expansion is amplifying the impact of global warming, resulting in more frequent and severe heatwaves in cities like Dubai and New Delhi.

Regional Temperature Patterns and Trends

  • Africa shows the highest average temperatures compared to other regions, which remains consistently higher than others throughout the years. This highlights the ongoing warmth in sub-Saharan and North African regions, driven by both geographic and climatic factors.
  • The hottest countries like Mali, Mauritania, and Burkina Faso all have temperatures averaging around 28Â°C or higher. These countries are located in Africa's Sahel and desert regions, which experience extreme heat due to both geographic location (near the equator) and limited urban infrastructure to mitigate heat. The United Arab Emirates stands out in the list, showcasing high temperatures due to rapid urbanization and desert climate conditions.
  • Oceania and South America show relatively stable temperatures over the past decades, with no drastic increases. This suggests more moderate temperature patterns in these regions compared to Africa and Asia

Urban Population Percentage vs Avg Temperature

Insights

Urban Population and Temperature Correlation

  • In Africa, the relationship between urbanization and average temperature appears weak, as seen in countries like Burkina Faso and Nigeria. Despite increasing urbanization, the temperature in these regions remains relatively stable, suggesting that climate factors such as geography, elevation, and climate may play a larger role than urbanization in determining local temperatures.
  • Asia (particularly India, China, and Indonesia) displays a wide range of urbanization and temperature combinations. Countries with high urbanization (like China) show a moderate temperature increase, while less urbanized countries like Nepal and Pakistan have lower temperatures. This highlights that regional factors such as industrialization and urban planning influence how urbanization impacts temperature, with some urbanized areas in Asia mitigating heat through cooling technologies and green infrastructure.
  • Australia and New Zealand are notable outliers, where high urbanization levels do not correspond to high average temperatures. This might be due to climate regulation mechanisms such as cooling technologies, green spaces, and milder overall climates in these regions, which help maintain a balance between urban growth and temperature control.
